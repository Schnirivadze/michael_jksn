1958 Childhood and early career in the group
29 August

Michael Jackson was born on August 29, 1958 in the town of Gary, Indiana, he became the seventh of nine children in the family of Joseph and Katherine Jackson

1960

Members of the Jackson family were adherents of the religion of Jehovah's Witnesses. Katherine began attending their church and was baptized in 1960 when Michael was 2 years old. The Jacksons took religious traditions very seriously, studied the Bible, attended Witness meetings, and did not celebrate Christmas or birthdays. Despite this, Michael was the only one in the family who was baptized after his mother. -->

During the 1960s. Joseph began to think about how to create a team consisting of his eldest sons. Initially, The Jackson Brothers consisted of Jackie, Jermaine, and Tito. Michael was still too young and only from the side watched how the older brothers rehearsed.

1963

In 1963, he began studying at Garnett Elementary School in Gary and performed at one of the school holidays with the song "Climb Ev'ry Mountain" ruen, then, noticing his talent, Catherine unexpectedly met a probably five-year-old boy in the lineup. Marlon joined the team at the same time, and the group of five groups was renamed The Jackson 5. The father raised the children, maintaining strict discipline, and used force in cases where something was not selective with him. Jackson grew up spending a lot of time in the studio, in rehearsals and meetings, the almost indistinguishable meaning of getting out and getting together with other children in the park. Despite the rough and oppressive methods of education, Michael always thanked his father for instilling in him the determination, in front of the public and accustomed him to strict discipline, which played a big role in Jackson's future success. At the age of 8, the boy became a two-soloist in the team.

1971

The group became more and more successful, but Michael attracted the most attention for his singing and choreography. As soon as the team gained recognition, the family moved from Indiana to Los Angeles, where they first lived in Gordy's house and Diana Ross's apartment. In 1971, the Jacksons purchased the Havenhurst estate in Encino and moved there.

1972 The beginning of a solo career
January

In January 1972, thirteen-year-old Michael Jackson released his first album, Got to Be There. The singer became the first artist on the Motown label to start a solo career without leaving the band. In the same year, Michael's second record, Ben, was released, the title track of the album became the soundtrack to the film of the same name, the song received the Golden Globe

The Jackson 5's relationship with Motown Records was becoming strained. The Jacksons wanted to be able to record and release songs of their own authorship, but Gordy did not see this as a good prospect, the compositions for the brothers were written by a group of authors and producers specially created in Motown called The Corporation. In the end, the Jackson family announced that they were leaving the label and entering into a contract with Epic Records, the group received a new name - The Jacksons.

1978
December

In December 1978, work began on the singer's new solo record, Off the Wall. Jackson wrote two songs for the album: "Don't Stop 'til You Get Enough" and "Working Day and Night". The list of songwriters also includes Stevie Wonder, Paul McCartney and Rod Temperton. Released on August 10, 1979 by Epic Records, 5 singles were released from the LP: "Don't Stop 'til You Get Enough", "Rock With You", "Off the Wall", "She's Out of My Life" and "Girlfriend", video clips were filmed for three of them. In the early 80s. Off the Wall became Michael's best-selling album at the time. The record was highly acclaimed by critics, with most reviewers agreeing that Jackson showed himself to be a grown-up, mature artist on it. The compositions in the album were sustained in the genres of rhythm and blues, funk, soul and disco. The first single from Off the Wall, "Don't Stop 'til You Get Enough", earned the singer his first Grammy and two American Music Awards. At the end of August 1979, Jackson turned 21, the contract between him and his father as a manager ended, and the singer gained independence from Joseph. Despite this, Michael continued to be the lead singer of The Jacksons, recording, producing the group's albums and going on concert tours in support of them.

1982 Leaving The Jacksons
April

Immediately after the release of Off The Wall, the singer began to prepare material for the next record. In April 1982, recording began on Jackson's new album, which later became known as Thriller. It was released on November 30, 1982, the first time CBS Records executives campaigned for a worldwide simultaneous release of the album, rather than the usual serial release. In just a year, Thriller became the best-selling album of all time and remains so to this day. According to various sources, global sales of Thriller range from 65 to 110 million copies.

1983
January

In early January 1983, the second single, "Billie Jean", was released, and in March, the music video for this song premiered on MTV. The video became the first music video by an African-American artist to get into the channel's hot rotation; up to this point, MTV executives had virtually never aired commercials of black artists.

November

In November 1983, together with his brothers, the singer signed a $ 5 million contract with PepsiCo, The Jacksons starred in a series of commercials. During the filming of one of them in early 1984, the musician received a scalp burn from the sparks of an early pyrotechnics, the singer needed a long recovery. The injury led to the appearance of painful scars - keloids, the situation was aggravated by discoid lupus erythematosus. Subsequently, Jackson suffered from excruciating pain for about 20 years. Pepsi paid the musician compensation in the amount of $ 1.5 million, the singer donated all the money to open a burn center

1984

In 1984, Jackson received seven Grammy statuettes for Thriller. In parallel with the preparation of the record, the musician worked on another project - an audiobook and soundtrack for Steven Spielberg's film "Alien", E.T. the Extra Terrestrial. The award for the song from the audiobook, "Someone in the Dark", was Jackson's eighth at the 1984 ceremony, setting the record for the most Grammys won in a single night.

A few months later, The Jacksons released the Victoryruen album, and the singer and his brothers went on their last joint tour of the United States and Canada. In November, the musician received a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame and became the first artist in history to have two stars on Vine Street - he received the first back in 1980 as part of The Jacksons. At the final show of the Victory tour in early December 1984, Michael announced that he was leaving the band.

1985 Purchase of the ATV catalog, "We Are the World", "Captain Io"

When, in 1981, Paul McCartney explained to Jackson how to profit from buying the publishing rights to songs, Paul McCartney became very interested in this idea and almost immediately began acquiring the rights to various compositions. The catalog, containing about 200 songs by The Beatles and approximately 4,000 compositions by other authors, was then owned by ATV Music Publishing. In 1981, McCartney and Yoko Ono made an attempt to buy The Beatles songs from there, but the then owner of ATV, Lew Grade, agreed to sell only the entire catalog at once for $ 30 million. As a result, the deal fell through as Ono and McCartney pulled out of the deal. The news that the ATV catalog was up for sale did not reach Jackson until September 1984. The musician expressed a desire to purchase it, and in order to avoid conflicts, the singer's lawyers contacted representatives of McCartney and Yoko Ono and received an answer that the purchase of the catalog was not planned in the near future. Before starting negotiations with the singer, the owners of the catalog also gave McCartney the first right to buy the rights, but he once again refused. Jackson's lawyers began a 10-month campaign to acquire the publishing rights, and, beating competitors, Jackson became the owner in mid-August 1985 for $47.5 million.

1987 Bad and Moonwalk

Since the mid 80s. Jackson began work on songs for the new record, he recorded his demos in his home studio in Encino in order to gain as much control as possible over the upcoming material, more creative freedom. The already far from ideal relationship between the musician and the producer stretched even more, and Bad became their third and last collaboration. It took the musician and producer 18 months to prepare the album and the promotional campaign for it, the release of the disc was postponed several times. Finally, in July 1987, the first single was released - "I Just Can't Stop Loving You" - and on August 31 the album was released. The list of songs on the disc included songs in the genres of funk, rhythm and blues, gospel, pop and hard rock, jazz, swing and blues. Reviews from critics ranged from "the singer's apparent growing confidence and autonomy as a producer and songwriter" to "the complete loss of Jackson's creative independence, stifled by the influence of Quincy Jones". The Thriller record by that time had become a kind of measure of the musician's creativity: most critics evaluated Jackson's subsequent albums, comparing them with her. Only a few reviewers noticed that it is incorrect to draw such analogies and consider each record separately.

2009 Death
25 June

Michael Jackson died on June 25, 2009 as a result of an overdose of drugs, in particular propofol. On the morning of June 25, 2009, doctor Conrad Murray injected Michael Jackson with propofol and left the singer alone. About 2 hours later, Murray returned to find his patient lying on the bed with his eyes and mouth wide open. The doctor tried to resuscitate the singer, but the attempts were unsuccessful. At 12:21 local Pacific time, a call to 911 was recorded. Arriving after 3 minutes and 17 seconds, the doctors found Jackson no longer breathing with a stopped heart and immediately began cardiopulmonary resuscitation. Efforts to bring Michael Jackson back to life continued along the way and for an hour after arriving at the UCLA Medical Center at 1:14 p.m. These attempts were unsuccessful. Death was pronounced at 14:26 local time. The news of his death received publicity in the first minutes after the incident.
